Community Foundation

Community Foundation is a digital platform for individuals within the local community, it enables users to be well integrated with activities and development of their community.

Problem Statement


Users are unable to navigate swiftly through pages due to the clutter of information and images and UI patterns that are not accessible therefore causing confusion and frustration.

The Idea


Our idea was to redesign the website's Information Architecture and improve the UI using standard patterns to enable users to access comprehensive sources of information about community activities, educational programmes and more.

Disciplines & Tools:


User Research (Interviews + Surveys) & Analysis, Ideating, Wireframing, User Testing, Creating Style Guide, Prototyping, Figma, Invasion.

Before Redesign

Research


First surveyd 10 potential audience of the Community Foundation
Second interviewed 8 individuals including the stakeholders.

To answer the following questions:

  1. How do Users feel about this website?
  2. What are users' pain points and frustrations interacting with the website?
  3. What are users' expectations from the current website?
  4. What services do users receive from the current website?

Affinity Diagram-main research findings


  1. The context doesn't have much structure
  2. The colours are not suitable which makes it hard to skim read.
  3. The website is a bit cluttered and inconsistent.
  4. It is not compatible with mobile devices.
  5. Video sections seem pretty confusing.

Problem Statement


We have observed that users find it hard to navigate the website, find it inaccessible and struggle to find the information they seek. How might we improve the Community Foundation website so that we can increase user satisfaction, attract more volunteers, receive more donations, and ultimately create a better user experience for the community.

User Persona


ANNIE ROMAN

Age: 24 Status: Single Location: Birmingham Occupation: Medical student

Goals:

To make new friends To improve her skills such as English, Confidence, etc. To help other people by volunteering and getting their help.

About:

Recently, Anne arrived in the UK to pursue her medical studies. She is experiencing loneliness and desires to form new connections and enhance her communication skills. Fortunately, a friend of hers introduced her to the community foundation organization website. This organization is composed of individuals from various backgrounds who support each other and offer opportunities for individuals to achieve their maximum potential.

Pain points:

Since she doesn't own a desktop system, she attempts to access the website using her mobile browser but soon realizes that the website is not compatible with mobile devices. The website is a bit cluttered, and it is not easy to find the required information. The font sizes and background colours are not suitable, making it difficult for her to read the context. Video sections seem pretty cluttered and confusing.

Summary and Takeaways

  • Time was greatly spent on the IA (Information Architecture) because clarifications had to be sought from the stakeholders regarding terms used (services and project) and then reiteration was made to provide a clear navigation.
  • User testing and Iterating the user flow and site map enabled us to understand users need and task goals for the platform.
  • Accessibility was greatly thought of throughout the redesign, such as the typography, colour and UI pattern placements.
  • Future developments to include a profile page which allows user to track their progress of programmes they have enrolled on.
